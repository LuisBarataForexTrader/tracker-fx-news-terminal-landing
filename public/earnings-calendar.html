FAQ

Earnings Calendar — Frequently Asked Questions

What is an earnings calendar?

An earnings calendar is a schedule of upcoming corporate earnings report dates. It shows when each company will release its quarterly results — including EPS (earnings per share) estimates, revenue expectations and whether the report comes before market open (BMO) or after market close (AMC). Traders use earnings calendars to prepare for potential price gaps and volatility spikes, and to track actual versus estimated figures the moment results are released — giving them an edge in the crucial seconds after a print.

How do earnings affect stock prices?

Earnings reports are among the most powerful single-event catalysts for individual stock prices. A significant EPS beat — especially combined with raised guidance — can gap a stock up 5–15% overnight. A miss or lowered guidance can trigger an equivalent selloff. Even when EPS meets expectations, the forward guidance language in the earnings call can move a stock sharply in either direction. TNT's earnings calendar injects all news coverage the instant results are released so you can react before the full market has digested the report.

What is EPS beat vs miss?

EPS (Earnings Per Share) is a company's net profit divided by its shares outstanding. Each quarter, analysts publish consensus EPS estimates before the report. A 'beat' means the actual EPS exceeded the consensus estimate — historically associated with positive price reactions, though the reaction also depends on the magnitude of the beat and forward guidance. A 'miss' means EPS came in below expectations — often triggering a selloff. TNT color-codes every earnings result green for beat, red for miss, grey for in-line, allowing you to scan dozens of results instantly without calculation.

How do I track earnings releases in real time?

Trading News Terminal's earnings calendar shows every scheduled release with date, time, EPS estimate and revenue estimate pre-populated. The moment a company reports, the actual figures populate in real time with beat/miss color coding — no manual refresh needed. Simultaneously, the integrated news feed injects all earnings-related coverage for that stock, including analyst reactions, guidance commentary and sector implications, within seconds of the release. The squawk box also reads out the headline result audibly.

Which stocks have earnings this week?

The TNT earnings calendar displays the full week's schedule grouped by day, including pre-market (BMO) and after-hours (AMC) releases. Major earnings weeks — which occur during US reporting season in January, April, July and October — see hundreds of companies reporting.
