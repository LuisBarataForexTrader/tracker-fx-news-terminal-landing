Market Screener — Frequently Asked Questions

What is a market screener?

A market screener is a tool that filters and ranks financial instruments — stocks, forex pairs, crypto, indices — based on technical and fundamental criteria like RSI, price momentum, volume and news activity. Traders use screeners to identify opportunities across hundreds of assets without manually checking each one. TNT's screener covers six asset classes simultaneously in a single tabbed interface.

How do I screen forex pairs?

In Trading News Terminal's market screener, open the Forex tab to see all major, minor and exotic pairs ranked by RSI score, price change and news activity. You can sort any column and filter by signal type (Bullish, Bearish, Overbought, Oversold, Neutral) to narrow down actionable setups instantly. The screener refreshes in real time, so the rankings always reflect the latest market conditions.

What is RSI in a market screener?

RSI (Relative Strength Index) measures momentum on a 0–100 scale. In a screener, RSI above 70 flags overbought conditions — a potential reversal or momentum continuation. Below 30 flags oversold conditions. TNT's screener color-codes these signals — Bullish (RSI 50–70), Bearish (RSI 30–50), Overbought (70+), Oversold (sub-30) and Neutral — so you can spot extremes across all asset classes at a glance without calculating anything manually.

How do professional traders use screeners?

Professional traders use market screeners at the start of each session to identify the most active, trending or oversold instruments. They combine technical signals (RSI, momentum) with news activity to prioritise assets where price movement and fundamental catalysts align. TNT's screener integrates both dimensions in one view — you see which instruments have both a technical signal and surging news coverage — eliminating the need to cross-reference multiple platforms.
