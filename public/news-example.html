Fed Daly Says Economy Solid, Consumers Still Spending

📅 April 17, 2026 · 15:33 UTC
🏷 Central Banks · USD
📰 via Bloomberg
USD ▲ EUR/USD ▼ US 10Y ▲ S&P 500

San Francisco Fed President Mary Daly said the U.S. economy remains solid with consumers continuing to spend at a healthy pace, signalling that the path of Federal Reserve monetary policy remains uncertain as officials weigh geopolitical risk alongside domestic demand.

Speaking at a Bloomberg event, Daly noted that the war in Iran and the resulting oil price shock have made the outlook for Fed policy uncertain during her remarks — a cautious tone that suggests the central bank is prepared to remain data-dependent.

Market reaction was measured: the U.S. Dollar firmed modestly against major peers, 10-year Treasury yields ticked higher, and equity futures held recent gains as traders digested the dovish-but-careful framing from the San Francisco Fed chief.
